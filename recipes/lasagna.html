Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish that embodies layers of flavor, texture, and heartwarming comfort At its core, lasagna is a baked casserole featuring sheets of pasta interspersed with rich, savory fillings Traditional lasagna often starts with a hearty meat sauce, such as a slow-simmered Bolognese, layered with creamy ricotta cheese mixed with herbs, gooey melted mozzarella, and a dusting of nutty Parmesan Each bite offers a perfect harmony of tender pasta, flavorful sauce, and indulgent cheese, creating a symphony of taste that's both satisfying and deeply comforting

Beyond its classic form, lasagna is incredibly versatile and can cater to various dietary preferences Vegetable lasagnas replace meat with roasted zucchini, spinach, or eggplant, often paired with a tangy tomato sauce or velvety béchamel Meanwhile, modern twists might include layers of pesto, seafood, or even a plant-based filling for a lighter yet equally delicious experience Its golden, bubbling crust and fragrant aroma make lasagna a centerpiece for family gatherings or casual weeknight dinners, delivering a sense of home with every serving

Ingredients:

  • Ground Beef
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • Tomato Sauce
  • Crushed tomatoes
  • Tomato paste
  • Ricotta cheese
  • mozzarella cheese
  • Parmesan Cheese
  • Eggs
  • Olive Oil
  • Seasoning
  1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet
  2. Sauté diced onion and minced garlic until fragrant
  3. Add ground meat (beef, pork, or turkey) and cook until browned
  4. Stir in tomato sauce, crushed tomatoes, and seasonings (Italian seasoning, salt, pepper, and optional red pepper flakes)
  5. Simmer on low heat for 15–20 minutes
  6. Boil lasagna noodles in salted water according to package instructions
  7. Drain and lay flat on parchment paper to prevent sticking
  8. In a mixing bowl, combine ricotta cheese, an egg, grated Parmesan, and chopped parsley or basil
  9. Mix until smooth and set aside
  10. Set the oven to 375°F (190°C)
  11. Spread a thin layer of meat sauce on the bottom of a baking dish
  12. Add a layer of lasagna noodles, slightly overlapping them
  13. Spread a layer of ricotta mixture over the noodles
  14. Sprinkle shredded mozzarella cheese on top
  15. Add another layer of meat sauce
  16. Repeat the layers (noodles, ricotta, mozzarella, meat sauce) until the dish is filled, ending with a layer of sauce and mozzarella on top
  17. Cover the dish with aluminum foil (spray the underside with cooking spray to prevent sticking)
  18. Bake for 25 minutes covered, then remove the foil and bake for an additional 20 minutes until the cheese is bubbly and golden
  19. Remove the lasagna from the oven and let it rest for 10–15 minutes before slicing This helps it set and makes serving easier
  20. Slice the lasagna into portions and serve hot with a side of garlic bread or salad
